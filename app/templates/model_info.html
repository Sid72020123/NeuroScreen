Model Architecture & Statistics

Academically proven metrics and engineering decisions for the NeuroScreen diagnostic system.

Dual-Modality Architecture

NeuroScreen utilizes a multi-modal system with two distinct models operating in parallel to evaluate motor and acoustic biomarkers independently. With an average ~84.6% accuracy and an exceptionally high Clinical Sensitivity (>81%), the triage system successfully keeps False Negatives to an absolute minimum.

Engineering Architecture

  • SHAP Feature Pruning: Mathematically proved that 5 of the 22 audio features (e.g., Jitter:DDP, MDVP:Shimmer) were confusing the model. Dropping them down to a clean 17-feature XGBoost architecture drastically reduced background noise.
  • Custom Thresholding: Manually shifted the decision boundary down to 0.39. This optimization perfectly balanced the model to prioritize catching sick patients (yielding 98.92% recall) without destroying overall correctness.
  • 5-Fold Cross-Validation: Tested the model across 5 different slices of the dataset, proving the 86.95% accuracy is a consistent, stable baseline. (Hold-out sets aligned perfectly at 85.71%).

Engineering Architecture

  • Kinematic-Safe Augmentation: Multiplied the data-hungry 734-image dataset through strict mathematically rotated (up to 10°) and flipped images. Avoiding "stretching" ensured the model learned true micro-tremors, not digital distortion.
  • Deeper Fine-Tuning: Unlocked the top 50 layers of the MobileNetV2 base architecture (up from 20). Combined with a dynamic ReduceLROnPlateau learning rate, this provided the exact mathematical flexibility needed to recognize high-frequency stuttering in pen strokes.
